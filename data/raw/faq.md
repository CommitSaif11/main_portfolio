## Biggest challenge you've faced in a project

A concrete example: on SiliconSeal, OCR readings off physical IC chips are never perfect — glare, wear, and angle mean the extracted text is often slightly off. A binary match/no-match check would have made the system unreliable. I ended up building a weighted confidence scoring system instead (part number, date code, and lot code each carry different weight based on how decisive and forgeable-relevant they are), so the system degrades gracefully instead of failing outright on imperfect input.

More broadly, that's the pattern across most of my harder problems: the highest-accuracy approach isn't always the best product decision. I've had to trade off performance, reliability, and explainability more than once, and building systems that hold up in the real world while still explaining their decisions has probably been my biggest recurring technical challenge — and honestly the part I enjoy most.
